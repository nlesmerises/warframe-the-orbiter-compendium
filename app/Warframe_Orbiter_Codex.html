🛸 1. What Is the Orbiter?

🛰️ Your Mobile Home Base

  • The Orbiter is your personal ship — the hub you return to between missions to gear up, craft, and manage everything you own.
  • It is run by your ship's AI, Cephalon Ordis, who narrates events and flags new items.
  • The small ship you deploy into missions is the Landing Craft (Liset, Mantis, etc.); the Orbiter is the larger vessel it docks with. Landing Craft choice affects your Air Support ability, not the Orbiter's stations.
  • The Orbiter grows over time: new consoles and rooms (“segments”) are added as you complete quests and progress — see the segment table at the bottom.

🧭 How to Use This Page

  • Each station below has a quick what it is / how you use it / when you care.
  • Where the compendium has a deep page for that activity, the card links straight to it — treat this as your “start here” map.
  • New player? Read top to bottom. Veteran? Jump to the station you need.

🗺️ 2. Navigation (Cockpit)

📋 What It Is

The console at the front of the Orbiter. Opens the Star Chart — where you pick planets, nodes, and mission types, and launch into play.

🔎 How You Use It & Where to Go

Plan your route through the system, unlock Junctions, and find mission types. Pair with the Progression Guide, the Quest Codex, and the Drop Tables to decide where to run for what you need.

🔧 3. The Stations — What Each One Does

⚔️ Arsenal

  • What: Equip and customize your Warframe, primary, secondary, melee, companion, and Archwing — plus mods and appearance.
  • Use it for: Loadouts, Forma, polarities, and cosmetics.
  • Go deeper: Builds Codex · Meta Weapons · Build Customizer

🔨 Foundry

  • What: Craft and claim everything — Warframes, weapons, gear, and resources from blueprints.
  • Use it for: Building new gear (watch build timers) and claiming finished items.
  • Go deeper: Foundry Planner · Foundry Farming · Weapon Recipes

🔢 Mods (Upgrade)

  • What: Manage your mod collection, fuse/rank mods with Endo, and apply them via the Arsenal.
  • Use it for: Ranking key mods and slotting Arcanes & Rivens.
  • Go deeper: Mods Database · Arcane Codex · Riven Guide

🧬 Helminth Infirmary

  • What: The Helminth segment — subsume a Warframe to learn an ability, then inject it onto another frame.
  • Use it for: Replacing a weak ability with a strong borrowed one (the heart of high-end builds).
  • Go deeper: Helminth Synergy App

🐾 Incubator

  • What: Breed and revive Kubrow and Kavat companions; manage genetic stabilizers.
  • Use it for: Hatching companions and swapping which one is active.
  • Go deeper: Companions & Vehicles

👀 Transference / Personal Quarters

  • What: The Somatic Link chair — switch to your Operator and access Focus schools (after The Second Dream / The War Within).
  • Use it for: Operator play, Focus, and Eidolon hunting.
  • Go deeper: Operator & Focus Guide

📢 Syndicate / Communications

  • What: Manage Syndicate allegiances, claim standing rewards, and read your Inbox from Ordis.
  • Use it for: Pledging syndicates and spending standing.
  • Go deeper: Syndicate Manager · Syndicate Farming

💰 Market

  • What: Buy blueprints, cosmetics, slots, and bundles — with credits or platinum.
  • Use it for: Warframe/weapon slots, Reactors/Catalysts, and starter blueprints.
  • Go deeper: Platinum Farming · Trading Ledger

🔬 Codex / Cephalon Simaris

  • What: Your scanned-enemy/lore database; Simaris targets give standing for scanning.
  • Use it for: Completing scans, buying Simaris offerings (e.g. Sculptures, mods).
  • Go deeper: Drop Tables for where things come from.

🚀 Railjack / Dry Dock

  • What: Access your Railjack (your crewed battleship) once unlocked; Cephalon Cy assists.
  • Use it for: Railjack missions, crew, and intrinsics.
  • Go deeper: Railjack Codex

🎨 Decoration / Captura

  • What: Decorate your Orbiter, place an Articula, play Somachord tracks, and stage photos with Captura.
  • Use it for: Personalization — no gameplay impact, pure home-making.
  • Go deeper: personal preference; nothing to farm here.

📧 Inbox (Ordis)

  • What: Story messages, event mails, and gift/reward attachments from Ordis and syndicates.
  • Use it for: Claiming attached rewards and starting quests delivered by mail — check it often.
