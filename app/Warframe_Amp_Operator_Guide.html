⚡ 1. Overview

Operator vs Drifter

Overview
  • Operator — the Tenno child; uses Amp and Void Mode; required for Eidolons, Zariman, Void content
  • Drifter — unlocked after The New War; uses Amp but different passive abilities; used in Duviri
  • Both use the same Amp — build choice carries over between them
  • Switch between Operator and Drifter in Operator Appearance settings

Why Upgrade Your Amp?

Overview
  • Mote Amp (starter) is too weak for Eidolons, Void Cascade, and Angel fights
  • Amp is the only tool that breaks Void shields on Eidolons and Sentients
  • Higher-tier Amps deal significantly more damage to Void-shielded enemies
  • Unlocks access to endgame open-world content effectively

🔧 2. Amp Parts

Part System (3-digit notation)

System
  • Amps are built from 3 parts — written as Prism-Scaffold-Brace (e.g. 177)
  • Prism — primary fire mode (1st digit)
  • Scaffold — secondary fire mode (2nd digit)
  • Brace — stat modifier: crit chance, status, energy regen (3rd digit)
  • Each digit = part tier from vendor (1 = Quills T1, 7 = Quills/Zariman T7)

Prism Types

Prism
  • 1 (Raplak) — semi-auto, high single-shot damage, good for Eidolon shields
  • 2 (Shwaak) — cone blast, crowd clear
  • 3 (Granmu) — grenade prism, AOE burst
  • 7 (Klamora) — sustained beam, best for general Void damage
  • X (Certus / Lega / Juttni) — Fortuna variants with status/crit focus

Scaffold Types

Scaffold
  • 1 (Pencha) — charged shot, high single-target
  • 7 (Propa) — sticky bomb alt-fire, excellent burst damage
  • Dissic — Fortuna scaffold, good area damage
  • Scaffold matters less than Prism — focus Prism choice first

🎯 3. Amp Builds by Use Case

Eidolon Hunting

Eidolons
  • 177 — Raplak Prism + Propa Scaffold + Certus Brace
  • Raplak (1) for fast, accurate Void shield breaks on Eidolon synovias
  • Propa (7) scaffold for burst damage on the exposed weak point
  • Certus Brace for high crit chance on the scaffold shot
  • Minimum viable: 223 — upgrade to 177 as soon as Quills rank allows

General Void Content (Zariman, Cascade)

Void
  • 777 — Klamora Prism + Propa Scaffold + Certus Brace
  • Klamora beam deals consistent Void damage to Manifestations and Angels
  • Good for Exolizer cleansing missions where sustained presence matters
  • Certus Brace maximizes critical output on scaffold

Starter / Budget

Budget
  • 223 — functional for early Eidolons; craft at Quills rank 1–2
  • Enough to break Teralyst shields reliably
  • Not sufficient for Garuda or Hydrolyst solo — upgrade to 177 before progressing
  • Get to Quills rank 2 (Observer) as fast as possible

🌟 4. Focus Schools

Madurai — Offense

Offense
  • Primary use: Eidolon shield breaking (Void Strike)
  • Void Strike: charge Void energy during Void Mode, release for massive Amp damage multiplier
  • Best school for Eidolon runs — mandatory for experienced hunters
  • Secondary: Way-Bound Madurai passives boost Warframe damage after Operator use

Vazarin — Support / Survival

Support
  • Void Radiance: heals allies on Operator dash — strong in squads
  • Protective Dash: makes allies invulnerable briefly on Operator dash
  • Best for Void Cascade — keeps team alive while cleansing Exolizers
  • Also useful for keeping Eidolon Lures alive during hunts

Zenurik — Energy

Energy
  • Energizing Dash: Operator dash leaves energy field, restores Warframe energy over time
  • Top passive for energy-hungry Warframes without Arcane Energize
  • Activate between Operator and Warframe use to keep abilities flowing
  • Recommended school if you haven’t yet farmed Arcane Energize R5

Naramon — Combo / Melee

Melee
  • Power Spike: melee combo counter decays slowly instead of resetting — major DPS boost
  • Best school for melee-focused builds maintaining high combo multipliers
  • Less useful for Void/Operator-focused content

Unairu — Defense / Strip

Defense
  • Wisp: Operator kills spawn a buff orb that strips enemy armor
  • Niche use: armor stripping on heavy Steel Path units
  • Least commonly used in endgame — Madurai/Vazarin/Zenurik cover most cases

🔆 5. Void Mode

What It Does

Overview
  • Pressing Operator key while Warframe is active switches to Operator (or Drifter)
  • Void Mode: hold crouch as Operator to become invisible and invulnerable to most damage
  • Void Mode drains energy — manage energy during extended use
  • Holding Void Mode while stationary builds Void Strike charge (Madurai)

Key Uses

Uses
  • Survive lethal damage by switching to Operator when Warframe is about to die
  • Break Void shields on Eidolons, Sentients, and Zariman Angels
  • Detect Voidplumes on the Zariman (glow visible in Void Mode)
  • Cleanse Exolizers in Void Cascade (must be in Void Mode while standing in them)
  • Revive a dead Warframe without a teammate using Operator survivability

💼 6. Crafting & Vendors

Quills (Cetus / Plains)

Vendor
  • Sell Prism, Scaffold, Brace parts for Tiers 1–3
  • Earn standing from Eidolon Sentient Cores (turn in after hunts)
  • Rank up: Unranked → Adherent → Observer → Instrument → Architect
  • Observer rank unlocks 223 parts; Architect unlocks 177

Vox Solaris (Fortuna / Orb Vallis)

Vendor
  • Sell Prism, Scaffold, Brace parts for Tiers 4–6 (Certus, Lega, Juttni, Dissic)
  • Earn standing from Toroids (farm near Fortuna caves and enemy camps)
  • Certus Brace (critical stats) sold here — essential for 177 build

Quinn / Holdfast (Zariman)

Vendor
  • Sell Tier 7 parts: Klamora Prism, Propa Scaffold, Lohrin Brace
  • Earn standing from Zariman bounties and Voidplume turn-ins
  • Lohrin Brace: status chance bonus — niche but viable for status Amp builds
