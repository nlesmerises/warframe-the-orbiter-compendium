🎰 1. What Is a Riven Mod?

🎲 Randomized, Weapon-Specific Mods

  • A riven is a special mod tied to one specific weapon (e.g. “Braton Riven”) with randomized stats.
  • Each riven rolls 2–3 positive stats and sometimes 1 negative (a negative often makes the positives stronger).
  • Riven stats stack with regular mods, so a good riven can push a weapon well past its normal ceiling.
  • Rivens exist for primaries, secondaries, melee, archguns, Kitguns, Zaws, and some companion weapons.

🔑 Veiled vs. Unveiled

  • Rivens drop veiled — unknown weapon and stats, with a challenge to complete.
  • Complete the challenge (or use a Riven Cipher to skip it) to unveil — this reveals the weapon and an initial random roll.
  • You then roll/re-roll with Kuva to change stats.
  • MR 8 is required to trade rivens.

⚖️ 3. Disposition — The Most Important Number

⚫ What Disposition Does

  • Disposition is a strength multiplier shown as 1–5 dots on the riven.
  • Higher disposition = bigger stat values from the same roll.
  • It is inversely tied to weapon popularity: weapons people use a lot get low disposition; rarely-used weapons get high disposition.
  • DE rebalances dispositions periodically (typically with major updates). A meta weapon's riven can lose value overnight when its disposition drops.

💡 What It Means For You

  • A high-disposition riven on a good weapon is the jackpot — and the most expensive.
  • Low-disposition rivens (popular meta guns) give smaller bonuses — often not worth a mod slot over a standard mod.
  • Before chasing a riven, ask: does the disposition make it beat my current build?

🌀 4. Rolling & Kuva

🎲 How Rolling Works

Re-rolling a riven costs Kuva and randomizes its stats again. The cost escalates with each roll (starting low, climbing to a fixed ceiling after ~13 rolls). Every roll can change the number of stats (2 vs 3), which stats appear, and whether there's a negative.

🔴 Farming Kuva

Kuva comes from Kuva Siphon / Kuva Flood missions (Kuva Fortress & the requiem-marked nodes), Steel Path bonuses, and resource boosters. Floods give the most per run. Stockpile before a rolling session — god rolls can take dozens of attempts.

📦 Riven Slots

Riven capacity is limited (15 by default) and expandable with platinum in increments. Veiled rivens you intend to sell still consume a slot — unveil/trade or dissolve duplicates to stay under the cap.

🛑 Know When to Stop

Decide your target stats before rolling. A “good enough” roll you keep beats a god roll you bankrupt yourself chasing. Lock in a 2-stat roll with no harmful negative as a solid baseline.

📖 5. Reading a Roll — Which Stats Matter

✅ High-Value Positives

  • Critical Damage and Critical Chance — king for crit weapons.
  • Multishot — near-universal damage multiplier.
  • Base Damage — always useful.
  • Elemental (Heat/Toxin/Electric/Cold) — for status/elemental builds.
  • Fire Rate / Attack Speed — situational but strong on the right gun.

⛔ “Harmless” Negatives (good to roll)

  • Zoom, Recoil, Punch Through, Ammo Max — rarely hurt; a negative here boosts your positives.
  • Damage to a faction you don't fight can be acceptable.
  • Avoid negatives to your core stat (e.g. −Critical Damage on a crit gun, −Multishot, −Damage).

Rule of thumb for a “god roll”: the two or three stats your build actually scales with, a harmless (or no) negative, on a weapon with decent disposition.

💰 Selling Smart

  • Price-check live before listing — riven prices swing with every disposition pass.
  • Veiled rivens for hyped weapons can be flipped without rolling at all.
  • Keep a couple of Riven Slivers banked; combining for a free veiled riven is steady value.
  • Log trades in the Trading Ledger and cross-reference Rare Mod Values.
