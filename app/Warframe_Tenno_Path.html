📖 1. Overview

What This Chapter Covers

Overview
  • Mastery Rank 1 through 10 — the entire new player foundation phase
  • How to level gear efficiently and earn Mastery XP as fast as possible
  • Which quests to prioritize and why
  • Your first real mods, weapons, and Warframes worth building
  • The habits that separate players who progress from players who stall

How MR Works

Mastery
  • Every weapon, Warframe, companion, and Archwing earns Mastery XP when leveled to rank 30 — but only the first time
  • Max rank a piece of gear, then sell or keep it — the XP is banked regardless
  • MR gates access to weapons, trading, and certain missions — higher MR opens more of the game
  • Run the MR test when ready — you can only attempt it once per day, so practice in Kiosks first
  • See the Mastery Rank Guide for full test breakdowns and XP requirements

The Golden Rule

Key Habit
  • Always be leveling something new. If everything in your loadout is already rank 30, you are not gaining MR XP
  • Keep a queue of unleveled gear in your Arsenal at all times
  • Use Hydron (Sedna) or Helene (Saturn) as your leveling ground once you can survive there
  • Bring a strong primary weapon at rank 30 to carry your unranked gear through missions

① 2. MR 1–4 — First Steps

Quests to Complete

Quests
  • Vor's Prize (auto-starts) — completes your tutorial, unlocks the Star Chart, gives you your first mod set and Orokin Reactor
  • Awakening — brief intro mission, unlocks basic navigation
  • Archwing — unlocks space combat; required for several planets and quests later
  • Once Awake — short quest, unlocks Alad V boss fight and key crafting materials
  • Check your Codex → Quests for available missions — do them as they unlock

Star Chart Priority

Navigation
  • Clear Earth → Venus → Mercury → Mars in order to open planet junctions
  • Each junction requires specific tasks (kill count, mod collection, etc.) — check the Junction screen
  • Do not skip planets — early planets drop essential mods and resources
  • Run Defense and Survival mission types for best early drop tables
  • Every node cleared counts toward your Star Chart completion (needed for Steel Path later)

Gear to Build First

Loadout
  • Craft Mk1-Braton or Braton (rifle) from the Market as your first primary if you want range
  • Farm the Rhino Warframe blueprint from the Jackal boss on Venus — Rhino is one of the strongest early frames and will carry you for dozens of hours
  • Build any weapons you can afford from the Market blueprints to stack MR XP fast
  • Level each piece to 30, then replace with the next tier
  • See the Meta Weapons Codex for tier lists by category

Resources to Gather

Farming
  • Ferrite (Earth) — used in almost every early craft
  • Polymer Bundle (Venus/Mercury) — needed for energy components
  • Circuits (Venus/Ceres) — used in nearly every Warframe component
  • Salvage (Mars/Jupiter) — large quantities needed for mid-game crafts
  • Run resource missions with a Resource Booster active if you have one from login rewards

② 3. MR 5–7 — Building Out

Quests to Complete

Quests
  • Stolen Dreams — unlocks Archwing mods and Void access
  • The New Strange — unlocks Chroma and Synthesis Scanner missions
  • Natah — major story quest, must be completed before The Second Dream
  • The Second Dream (MR 5 recommended) — the first major turning point of the game; unlocks Operator mode, Void Mode, and Focus schools. Do not skip this
  • Complete Sortie prerequisite: finish all main story quests up to this point

Leveling Station: Hydron

XP Farm
  • Hydron (Sedna, Defense) is the fastest affinity farm in the game for new players
  • Bring one rank-30 weapon to contribute damage, and fill the rest of your loadout with unranked gear
  • Squad up — affinity sharing means everyone benefits from kills even if you're not the one doing damage
  • 10 waves nets significant XP; 20 waves is the sweet spot before diminishing returns
  • Alternatively use Helene (Saturn) if Hydron is too difficult early on

Mods: What to Prioritize

Mods
  • At this stage mods are your biggest power lever — a well-modded Mk1 beats a poorly modded Soma Prime
  • Serration (rifle damage +165% at max) — your single most important mod; start upgrading immediately
  • Hornet Strike (pistol damage) and Point Blank (shotgun damage) — same priority as Serration for their weapon types
  • Vitality (health) and Steel Fiber (armor) — survivability on your Warframe
  • See the Mods Database for full mod tier lists

Syndicates: Join One Now

Factions
  • Syndicates give powerful mods, weapons, and augments in exchange for Standing earned from missions
  • Pick one or two aligned syndicates from: Steel Meridian, Arbiters of Hexis, Cephalon Suda, The Perrin Sequence, Red Veil, New Loka
  • Equip their Sigil on your Warframe to earn standing during any mission
  • Daily standing cap scales with your MR — the sooner you start, the more you accumulate
  • See Systems & Progress for syndicate strategy

③ 4. MR 8–10 — First Real Arsenal

Quests to Complete

Quests
  • The War Within (MR 8 recommended) — unlocks full Operator combat, Void Mode, transference strikes, and Focus school upgrades. Required to access Kuva Fortress and much of the mid-game
  • Chains of Harrow — good story content, unlocks Harrow Warframe
  • Sands of Inaros — purchasable quest, rewards the extremely durable Inaros frame
  • Octavia's Anthem — unlocks Octavia, one of the strongest support frames in the game

First Open World: Plains of Eidolon

Open World
  • Access via Cetus on Earth — talk to Konzu for bounties
  • Bounties give Standing for the Ostron syndicate — used to buy Amp parts and unique mods
  • Complete the Saya's Vigil and Vox Solaris introductory quests to unlock full access
  • Do not worry about Eidolon hunts yet — these require a proper Amp build which comes in MR 11+
  • See the Open Worlds Guide for full Plains coverage

Corrupted Mods: Orokin Vaults

Mods
  • Corrupted mods are powerful double-edged mods that supercharge specific stats at the cost of another
  • Examples: Blind Rage (+ability strength, −efficiency), Fleeting Expertise (+efficiency, −duration)
  • Farm them by opening Orokin Vaults on Cambion Drift (Deimos) — each vault requires one of four Dragon Keys equipped
  • Bring a squad with all 4 key types to open all vaults in one run
  • These mods define most endgame Warframe builds — the sooner you start collecting them, the better

Void Relics: Prime Gear

Prime
  • Prime weapons and Warframes are significantly more powerful than their base versions — better base stats, more mod capacity, and Orokin Reactor/Catalyst built in
  • Collect Void Relics from missions, then crack them in Void Fissure missions to extract Prime parts
  • Trade with other players for Prime parts you're missing — see Void Relics Meta Guide
  • Priority Prime targets at this stage: Rhino Prime, Soma Prime, Orthos Prime

🔧 5. Priority Mods for MR 1–10

Warframe Mods

Frame
  • Vitality — +440% health at max rank; your most important survivability mod
  • Steel Fiber — +110% armor; works best on high-armor frames like Rhino and Inaros
  • Redirection — +440% shields; better on low-armor frames like Mag and Nova
  • Streamline — −30% ability energy cost; lets you cast more often
  • Intensify — +30% ability strength; core ability mod for casters
  • Flow — +150% energy max; pairs with Streamline for sustained casting

Primary Weapon Mods

Weapons
  • Serration — +165% damage; max rank this as soon as possible, it costs a lot of Endo
  • Split Chamber — +90% multishot (fires an extra projectile); effectively doubles DPS
  • Point Strike — +150% critical chance; essential for crit-based rifles
  • Vital Sense — +120% critical damage multiplier
  • Two elemental damage mods (e.g. Hellfire + Infected Clip = Radiation) — match elements to enemy faction
  • See Damage Guide for faction matchups

Where to Get Them

Sources
  • Serration, Vitality, Redirection — drop from nearly any enemy; also sold in the Market
  • Split Chamber — drops from Corrupted Heavy Gunner (Orokin Derelict) or traded from players
  • Streamline, Intensify, Continuity — Nightmare mode mission rewards (unlocked after completing a planet)
  • Flow — drops from Orokin Tower missions (Void)
  • Buy Endo from Maroo's Bazaar weekly Ayatan missions to rank up mods faster

⚠️ 6. What to Avoid at This Stage

Common New Player Mistakes

Avoid
  • Buying Warframes with Platinum — almost every frame can be farmed in-game; save Platinum for slots and cosmetics
  • Ignoring mod capacity — an unmodded rank-30 weapon is weaker than a well-modded rank-0 weapon
  • Skipping Orokin Reactors and Catalysts — always install these on gear you plan to keep; they double mod capacity
  • Selling mods you think are useless — many mods seem bad early but are essential in builds later
  • Rushing the star chart without clearing nodes — every uncleared node is locked MR progress later

Platinum: What to Spend It On

Economy
  • Warframe slots — you start with 2; buy more as you build new frames (50 Platinum for 2 slots)
  • Weapon slots — same priority; you need slots before you can equip new gear
  • Orokin Reactor / Catalyst (if not available from alerts) — 20 Platinum each, worth every credit
  • Do NOT spend on: Warframes (farmable), weapons (farmable), booster packs (poor value), cosmetics until you're stable

🌟 7. What’s Next — MR 11–20 Expansion

You’re Ready for Chapter 2 When…

Checklist
  • You have reached MR 10 or higher
  • The War Within quest is complete and your Operator is active
  • You have a reliable meta primary weapon at rank 30 with full mods
  • Serration, Split Chamber, and Vitality are all at or near max rank
  • You have at least one syndicate earning standing for you every session
  • Your Rhino (or equivalent tank frame) is fully built and modded
