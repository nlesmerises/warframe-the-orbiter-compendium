⚔️ 1. Overview

What Is Conclave?

Overview
  • Warframe's dedicated Player vs. Player (PvP) mode — entirely separate from PvE gameplay
  • Hosted by the Conclave Syndicate, led by Teshin — accessible from any Relay
  • All abilities and weapons are rebalanced for PvP — stats, damage, and mechanics differ significantly from PvE versions
  • Introduced as a permanent alternative game mode; population is low but a dedicated community exists
  • The primary reason most players engage with Conclave is to farm exclusive cosmetics and mods unavailable anywhere else

Honest State of Conclave (2026)

Overview
  • Population is sparse — matchmaking can take several minutes on most platforms; finding a full lobby is rare
  • The community is small but enthusiastic; experienced players dominate most lobbies
  • DE has made limited investment in Conclave development in recent years — expect minimal updates
  • If you want the exclusive rewards (skins, sigils, mods), plan for a longer grind than PvE equivalents
  • Cross-save has not meaningfully fixed population fragmentation across platforms
  • Solo farming is possible for some standing — see tips in the Syndicate section

🔒 2. Access & Setup

Getting Started

Access
  • Visit Teshin in any Relay — he is in the back-left corridor near the Conclave terminal
  • No quest or MR requirement to enter — Conclave is accessible from early in the game
  • From the Conclave terminal, browse active lobbies or create your own session
  • You can set matches to private or invite-only — useful for farming with friends or a premade group
  • Standing gain from private/invite-only matches is reduced compared to public matches

Loadout Restrictions

Access
  • Only weapons and Warframes with a Conclave rating can be used in PvP
  • Most standard weapons are allowed; Riven-modded, Kuva, and Tenet weapons have restricted or adjusted stats
  • Mods have PvP-specific values — a mod that gives +165% damage in PvE may give +20% in Conclave
  • Dedicated Conclave mods (earned via Conclave standing) are often more effective in PvP than standard mods
  • Check the mod description's PvP icon to see its Conclave-specific stat value

Conclave-Specific Mods

Access
  • Purchasable from Teshin with Conclave Standing at various ranks
  • Categories: weapon damage, reload, movement (parkour speed, aim glide), ability tweaks, survivability
  • Some are PvE-legal too — Enduring Paralysis (Ash augment), Electromagnetic Shielding, etc. are used in PvE builds
  • Worth farming standing specifically for PvE-useful Conclave mods even if you don't play PvP long-term
  • Full mod list available from Teshin's standing store sorted by rank requirement

🎮 3. Game Modes

🔴 Annihilation (Deathmatch)

Modes
  • Free-for-all deathmatch — every player for themselves
  • First player to reach the kill threshold wins; if time expires, most kills wins
  • No teams; full chaos; good for learning PvP movement and aiming without team coordination
  • Most common mode for solo queueing — lower player counts still function (4-player minimum)
  • Standing gain: per kill + bonus for finishing in top half

👥 Team Annihilation

Modes
  • 4v4 team deathmatch — first team to reach the kill threshold wins
  • Coordinated teamplay matters — flanking, cover fire, and ability synergies are viable
  • Reviving teammates is possible — a downed player becomes a slow target; reviving costs time but denies the kill
  • Best mode for premade groups — communication via squad turns this into a genuine tactical experience
  • Standing gain: per kill + win bonus

🎯 Capture the Cephalon

Modes
  • Team objective mode — capture the enemy Cephalon and return it to your base while defending your own
  • Direct equivalent of Capture the Flag — the Cephalon carrier is slowed and can be killed to drop it
  • High teamwork requirement — needs dedicated carriers, defenders, and interceptors
  • Most fun in a coordinated premade; chaotic in a random lobby
  • Standing gain: per capture + win bonus; higher per-match ceiling than deathmatch

🏠 Cephalon Capture (Solo/Mixed)

Modes
  • Collect Cephalon fragments scattered across the map — each fragment is worth points
  • Fragments are dropped by killed players and must be physically carried to a deposit point
  • A hybrid of deathmatch and objective — aggressive players farm kills, strategic players farm deposits
  • Good standing gain when played aggressively with a fast-moving frame

🏭 Lunaro (Sports Mode)

Modes
  • A unique ball-sport mode — no weapons, no killing; score by launching the Lunaro ball into the opposing team's goal
  • Abilities are severely restricted; movement and positioning are the primary skills
  • Niche but beloved by a small dedicated community; very different feel from other Conclave modes
  • Standing gain is modest — Lunaro is primarily played for fun, not standing efficiency
  • The Lunaro community occasionally organizes scheduled matches — check community Discord servers

⚙️ 4. PvP Mechanics

Health & Damage

Mechanics
  • All Warframes have normalized health and shields in Conclave — your PvE stats do not carry over
  • Damage is heavily reduced versus PvE — weapons that one-shot enemies in PvE may do 5–20% of a player's health per shot
  • Status effects work differently: most procs deal less damage, shorter duration, or are fully disabled in PvP
  • Headshots deal bonus damage — aim for the head, especially with slower-firing weapons
  • Stamina exists in Conclave for blocking and certain movement actions — manage it during fights

Movement & Mobility

Mechanics
  • PvP rewards aggressive, mobile play — staying still is a death sentence
  • Bullet jumping, wall-running, and aim glide are your primary survival tools; use them constantly
  • Directional bullet jumps change your velocity unpredictably — hard to track for opponents
  • Sprint speed and parkour mods matter more in Conclave than in PvE — equip dedicated movement mods
  • Vertical movement (wall runs, double jumps) is heavily underused by new players — climb and attack from above

Abilities in PvP

Mechanics
  • Abilities are rebalanced individually — some are strong, many are nerfed to near-uselessness
  • Hard CC (sleep, freeze, full stun) is removed or has very short duration to prevent instant-kill chains
  • Movement abilities (Volt Speed, Zephyr Tail Wind, Wisp Wil-o-Wisp) retain utility for repositioning
  • Defensive abilities (Rhino Iron Skin, Mesa Ballistic Battery) are reduced in value
  • Damage abilities deal only a fraction of their PvE damage — kills via abilities alone are rare
  • Best approach: treat abilities as supplements to weapon kills, not replacements

Tips for New PvP Players

Mechanics
  • Expect to lose — a lot — at first; the Conclave skill floor is high for veteran players
  • Focus on movement first: never stop moving, use every parkour trick available
  • Pick a simple, responsive weapon for learning — hitscan weapons (rifles, pistols) are easier to land than projectiles
  • Use cover and vertical elevation; attack from unexpected angles
  • Avoid bringing complex ability builds — simple, durable frames (Rhino, Excalibur) are better for learning
  • Standing is earned even from losses — don't quit; every match awards some standing

🛠️ 5. Builds & Loadouts

Recommended Frames for Beginners

Builds
  • Excalibur — Exalted Blade has PvP viability; Slash Dash is a fast gap-closer; well-rounded and beginner-friendly
  • Rhino — Iron Skin absorbs some hits; straightforward survivability; good for learning while taking hits
  • Volt — Speed boosts mobility and escape; Electric Shield provides minor cover; quick playstyle
  • Ash — Teleport for rapid repositioning; Bladestorm is nerfed but Smoke Screen gives brief evasion
  • Avoid squishy casters (Saryn, Octavia) until you understand the movement game

Competitive Frame Picks

Builds
  • Zephyr — Tail Wind gives exceptional aerial mobility; very hard to track mid-air; top movement frame in PvP
  • Loki — Invisibility works in PvP (shorter duration); Radial Disarm has limited effect; top stealth frame for ambush play
  • Valkyr — Hysteria is usable in PvP; high melee output; aggressive close-range playstyle
  • Wukong — Cloud Walker grants brief invulnerability; strong for disengaging from bad fights
  • Titania — Razorwing miniature form is a tiny, fast-moving target; extremely hard to hit

Weapon Recommendations

Builds
  • Hitscan primaries (Strun Wraith, Soma, Paris Prime) — consistent damage; no projectile travel time to account for
  • Shotguns — very strong at close range in PvP; Strun Wraith and Kohm variants shred in melee range
  • Bows (Paris Prime, Dread) — high burst damage if you can land shots; rewarding for experienced players
  • Secondaries — Lex Prime, Euphona Prime, and Furis variants are top picks; high damage-per-shot for close engagements
  • Melee — Nikana, Orthos Prime, and fast-attacking swords work well; block cancels are important for melee dueling
  • Avoid slow projectile launchers (Ogris, Zarr) — experienced players dodge them easily

Key Conclave Mods to Equip

Builds
  • Mobilize (Exilus mod) — reduces aim glide drain; lets you stay airborne longer and aim from height
  • Constitution — faster recovery from knockdowns; critical for staying on your feet
  • Directed Convergence — faster aiming down sights; important for hitscan weapons
  • Evasion — brief dodge invulnerability frames; strong defensive mod
  • Conclave-specific elemental mods — have higher PvP values than standard elemental mods; replace standard mods with these as you earn them

🏆 6. Conclave Syndicate & Rewards

Syndicate Ranks

Syndicate
  • Five ranks with Teshin: Neutral → Typhoon → Hurricane → Tornadic → Tempest → Typhoon Elite (exact names vary by display)
  • Standing is earned from kills, assists, objectives, and match wins in any Conclave mode
  • Each rank unlocks new items in Teshin's store — cosmetics, mods, and decorations gate behind rank
  • Daily standing cap applies — same formula as other syndicates (MR × 1,000 base)
  • Private lobbies with friends still award standing, but at a reduced rate

Exclusive Cosmetics

Syndicate
  • Conclave Sigils — rank-gated sigils; some are visually distinct and cannot be obtained any other way
  • Conclave Skins — several Warframe skins are Conclave-exclusive (Teshin's Graxx armor sets, Conclave-themed accessories)
  • Conclave Noggle (Teshin Noggle) — decorative figure; standing purchase
  • Stencils & Emblems — minor cosmetics for Orbiter decoration
  • Cosmetics are permanently account-bound once purchased — no time limit to use them

Exclusive & PvE-Useful Mods

Syndicate
  • Enduring Paralysis (Ash augment) — extends Ash's Smoke Screen stun; used in PvE Ash builds
  • Electromagnetic Shielding — Shield Gating mod with PvE applications
  • Mobilize — aim glide duration increase; widely used in PvE mobility builds
  • Prepare for Glory — Valkyr augment with PvP and niche PvE uses
  • Mod Archive — several unique Conclave mods have PvE crossover value; check Teshin's full store list
  • Target these mods specifically to minimize required standing grind if you dislike PvP

Standing Farming Strategy

Syndicate
  • Public Annihilation — queue in, earn standing per kill even in losses; fastest public standing gain
  • Private match with a friend — create a session, invite a friend, and take turns earning kills; slower but reliable if public queues are dead
  • Lunaro private — if you only want standing and don't enjoy combat, Lunaro with a friend can be farmed by scoring repeatedly
  • Reach the first two syndicate ranks for the most-wanted PvE mods (Mobilize, Electromagnetic Shielding) — this is the minimum grind if you just want those
  • Full Tempest rank is a long-term project; plan several weeks of daily cap farming
