🌀 1. What Is Duviri?

A Self-Contained Realm

Overview
  • Duviri is a separate open landscape you play as the Drifter (not your Warframe), opened by The Duviri Paradox quest — see the Quest Codex.
  • It can be played very early — it's one of the few zones reachable almost from the start.
  • You begin most runs with no mods or arsenal; power comes from Decrees you collect during the run (a roguelike loop).
  • Ruled by the mad king and tied to Teshin's Cave; reachable from Navigation.

Why Play It

Overview
  • Incarnon Adapters for classic weapons (via the Steel Path Circuit).
  • Kullervo the Warframe, Pathos Clamps, and assorted resources.
  • Relaxed, gear-agnostic gameplay — good when you don't want to manage a loadout.
  • Drifter Intrinsics progression unique to Duviri.

🎭 2. The Spiral & Its Moods

The world runs on a cycle of five emotional moods (the Spiral). The active mood rotates on a real-time timer (about every couple of hours) and changes enemy behavior, the environment, and some activities. You can't pick the mood — you adapt to whichever is active.

🌟 The Five Moods

  • Joy — upbeat, playful encounters
  • Anger — aggressive, harder-hitting enemies
  • Envy — thieving/decoy themes
  • Sorrow — somber, slower tone
  • Fear — oppressive, defensive pressure

🔎 What It Affects

  • Enemy aggression and which foes appear
  • The feel of the open world and side activities
  • Some reward flavor — but core rewards come from completing content, not the mood
  • It's cosmetic-to-tactical, not a hard gate — just play to the mood

📜 3. Decrees (Roguelike Buffs)

📋 How They Work

Decrees are temporary buffs you collect during a run — from clearing Undercroft stages and completing feats. They stack and can snowball you into enormous power by the end of a session.

🎣 The Catch

Decrees reset when you leave Duviri — they don't carry over. The loop is: enter weak, build up Decrees, get strong, finish the run. In the Circuit, Decrees also carry your chosen Warframe instead of the Drifter.

🔄 4. Two Modes: Experience vs. Circuit

The Duviri Experience

Story Mode
  • The story/open mode — play as the Drifter across the landscape.
  • Side objectives, mood encounters, and the Orowyrm dragon boss fight.
  • Rewards Pathos Clamps (from the Orowyrm) and Duviri resources.

The Circuit

Warframe Mode
  • The Warframe mode — you pick from offered frames/weapons and push through rounds, gaining Decrees.
  • Normal Circuit: rewards Incarnon Adapters for classic weapons (weekly rotation).
  • Steel Path Circuit: rewards Melee Arcanes and harder-tier loot.
  • Track it: Circuit Tracker · Incarnon Tracker

📎 5. Pathos Clamps & Kullervo

💰 Pathos Clamps

Earned mainly by defeating the Orowyrm. The premium Duviri currency — spent at Kullervo's Hold on Kullervo's parts and other offerings (items typically cost ~10–20 Clamps apiece).

👑 Kullervo (Warframe)

A Duviri-exclusive frame — buy his blueprint and parts at Kullervo's Hold with Pathos Clamps, then build in the Foundry. A strong melee/burst frame worth the farm.

⭐ 6. Drifter Intrinsics

Duviri has its own progression: four Drifter Intrinsic trees, each maxing at rank 10. You earn Intrinsic points from Duviri activities and spend them to improve the Drifter and your Duviri quality-of-life.

🔬 The Four Trees

  • Combat — Drifter melee/gunplay power and combos.
  • Riding — Kaithe (and K-Drive) mobility and mounted combat.
  • Opportunity — rewards, resource pickups, and utility.
  • Endurance — Drifter survivability and health/recovery.

🎯 Priorities

  • Early ranks in Combat and Endurance make solo runs much smoother.
  • Riding speeds up traversal of the large map.
  • Opportunity pays off once you're farming rewards regularly.

💡 7. Practical Tips

  • For Incarnon weapons, run the normal Circuit weekly and pick the adapter you want from the rotation — see the Incarnon Tracker.
  • For Melee Arcanes, run the Steel Path Circuit — cross-referenced in the Arcane Codex.
  • Lean into Decrees — grab every one offered; a good Decree run trivializes the Orowyrm.
  • Don't fight the mood — adapt; rewards don't depend on getting a "good" one.
  • Chip away at Drifter Intrinsics as you go; they make every future run easier.
