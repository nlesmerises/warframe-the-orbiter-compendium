🔴 1. Disruption

How It Works

Disruption
  • Defend four Conduit terminals spread across the map — each conduit is activated by inserting a Demolyst key
  • Killing enemies drops Demolyst keys; pick one up and carry it to an active conduit to start the sequence
  • Once a conduit is active, a Demolyst enemy spawns — a heavily armored unit on a countdown that will destroy the conduit if it reaches it
  • Kill the Demolyst before it reaches the conduit to protect it; if it detonates, the conduit is lost for that round
  • Complete a round by resolving all four conduits (protected or lost). Lost conduits trigger negative effects (see below). Repeat rounds for better rotation rewards

Demolyst Modifiers (Conduit Lost)

Disruption
  • Malicious Spread — rapidly spreads Infestation across the tile; deal 50% reduced damage in infected zones
  • Electromagnetic Disturbance — disables abilities for the entire team for ~30 seconds
  • Energy Drain — rapidly drains all energy to zero
  • Marked for Death — random player is hunted by a powerful assassination target
  • You can still complete the mission with some conduits lost — but aim for all 4 protected per round for best rewards

Build Recommendations

Disruption
  • Demolysts are immune to crowd control — no freezing, no sleep, no knockdown
  • They have high armor — use Corrosive + Heat or Viral + Slash for fast kills
  • Speed is everything: Gauss, Volt, Wisp (Haste mote) or Titania all help intercept Demolysts quickly
  • Strong single-target DPS frames (Mesa, Saryn, Khora) work well for killing Demolysts before they close
  • Bring energy restores or Energize arcane — keeping abilities active between rounds matters

Key Disruption Nodes & Rewards

Disruption
  • Olympus, Mars — earliest access; early Disruption rewards
  • Kappa, Sedna — high-level; Harrow Neuroptics, Systems, Chassis
  • Ur, Uranus — Gauss Neuroptics, Chassis, Systems
  • Ganymede, Jupiter — Atlas Neuroptics, Chassis, Systems
  • Lua Disruption — Acrithis vendor bounties; Lua-specific arcanes
  • Rotation C (4 conduits protected × 2 rounds) gives the rarest drops — target this for Warframe parts

🆑 2. Elite Sanctuary Onslaught (ESO)

How It Works

ESO
  • Wave-based survival variant hosted by Cephalon Simaris — accessed via the Syndicate terminal on any Relay
  • Kill enemies to fill the Efficiency meter; if Efficiency drops to zero the mission ends
  • Each zone lasts 2.5 minutes; portals open at the end — enter to advance to the next zone
  • Enemies scale rapidly — by zone 4–6 they are far above standard star chart levels
  • 8 zones total; most players extract at zone 4 (Rotation B) or zone 8 (Rotation D) for best reward value

Standard Sanctuary Onslaught vs ESO

ESO
  • Sanctuary Onslaught (SO) — normal difficulty; accessible earlier; good for leveling and Khora farming
  • Elite Sanctuary Onslaught (ESO) — requires MR 8+; enemies start at level 90+; much harder but better rewards
  • Both award Simaris Standing and use the same zone/efficiency structure
  • ESO is the primary source of Khora parts (SO also drops them but at lower rates)

Build Recommendations

ESO
  • AoE clear is king — Saryn (Spores + Miasma), Mesa (Peacemakers), Octavia (Mallet), Equinox (Maim)
  • Efficiency is maintained by kill rate — slow kills = failing efficiency; bring your highest-DPS build
  • Adaptation + high EHP recommended from Zone 4 onward — enemies hit hard
  • Energy sustain is critical: Zenurik school, Energize arcane, or Arcane Energize all viable
  • Avoid frames with slow kill setups — the efficiency drain waits for no one

Rewards

ESO
  • Khora Neuroptics (Zone 1), Chassis (Zone 2), Systems (Zone 3), Blueprint (Zone 4)
  • Simaris Standing — significant amount per zone; useful for Simaris store (Augment mods, Simulacrum)
  • Rotation C/D: Riven Slivers, Forma Blueprints, Endo bundles
  • Weekly Simaris standing cap is generous — ESO fills it faster than scanning
  • Simulacrum key (practice room) purchasable from Simaris after earning enough standing

💲 3. The Index

How It Works

The Index
  • A gladiatorial arena on Neptune hosted by Nef Anyo — PvE gambling mode for Credits
  • Your team bets Credits (Low: 30k, Medium: 70k, High: 105k) at the start
  • Kill enemies to collect Index Points (a score token) — deliver them to the goal post to score
  • First team to reach the point threshold wins; your wager is returned plus profit
  • You carry a personal debt when holding Index Points — the more you carry, the more damage you take

Credit Returns

The Index
  • Low Risk — bet 30,000 Cr, win ~75,000 Cr (net ~45k)
  • Medium Risk — bet 70,000 Cr, win ~175,000 Cr (net ~105k)
  • High Risk — bet 105,000 Cr, win ~265,000 Cr (net ~160k)
  • With a Credit Booster active: all returns double — High Risk nets ~320k per run
  • Fastest credit farm in the game with a good team; 3–4 min runs at High Risk

Build Recommendations

The Index
  • Enemies apply Robotic resistances — Radiation + Puncture or Corrosive are most effective
  • Nekros (with Despoil) is a strong pick — Desecrate adds extra looting without energy cost
  • Nova (Molecular Prime) slows enemies and multiplies damage — great for point denial
  • Rhino — Iron Skin absorbs the debt damage penalty; comfortable tanking with points
  • Mesa or any high-DPS frame shreds the arena quickly at High Risk
  • Carry as many points as you can safely hold before banking — fewer trips = faster runs

Tips & Tricks

The Index
  • The enemy team also scores — killing their point-carriers before they bank is just as important as scoring yourself
  • A dedicated point-carrier with high EHP (Inaros, Rhino) can hold 20+ points at once
  • Solo runs are viable — AI opponents and slower pacing make it manageable but slower
  • The Index is not affected by Resource or Affinity boosters — only Credit Booster matters here
  • Best time investment for raw credits outside of Arbitration credit caches

⚡ 4. Sorties

How It Works

Sorties
  • Three daily missions that must be completed in sequence — each mission has a condition modifier that adds challenge
  • Missions rotate daily at reset (same time as Nightwave); requires completion of The War Within to unlock
  • Enemy level: 50–100 in the first two missions; level 80–100 in the third
  • Each mission has one of ~20 possible condition modifiers (Eximus Stronghold, Augmented Enemy Armor, Energy Reduction, etc.)
  • One reward per day — you cannot replay for a different reward on the same day

Common Sortie Conditions

Sorties
  • Eximus Stronghold — unusually high density of Eximus units; bring high AoE DPS
  • Augmented Enemy Armor — all enemies have 2× base armor; use Armor Strip (Corrosive, Terrify, Roar)
  • Augmented Enemy Shields — Corpus only; use Magnetic or Toxin bypass
  • Energy Reduction — abilities cost 2× energy; bring Zenurik or Energize
  • Assault Rifle Only / Shotgun Only — weapon restriction; keep meta options in each category
  • Low Gravity — movement floats; can be fun or annoying depending on your frame
  • Sniper Rifle Only — Rubico Prime or Lanka shine here

Reward Pool

Sorties
  • Riven Mods (unrolled) — most common high-value reward; ~45% chance
  • Kuva (3,000–4,000 per reward) — consistent Riven reroll currency
  • Exilus Adapter Blueprint — moderate value; needed for Exilus slots
  • Nitain Extract — occasional; used in several Warframe blueprints
  • Ayatan Anasa Sculpture — moderate Endo value when filled
  • Legendary Core (rare) — instantly maxes any mod; extremely rare
  • 3-Day / 7-Day Boosters — Affinity, Credit, Resource; decent value

Tips

Sorties
  • Do Sorties every day — Riven accumulation over weeks is significant passive value
  • If you get a weapon-restriction condition, make sure you have a good build for that type before starting
  • Squad up — Sorties are designed for four players and go much faster with a team
  • Sorties are now superseded by Archon Hunts for top-tier daily rewards — but Sorties remain the best source of Riven Mods
  • Forma Blueprint reward is intentionally common filler — discard or use freely

🆕 5. Other Notable Mission Types

🛡️ Defense

Other Missions
  • Protect a Cryopod (or Operative) for wave-based rounds; extract every 5 waves
  • Rewards rotate on a 5-wave (A/B/C) cycle — stay for Rotation C (wave 10, 20, etc.) for best drops
  • Hydron, Sedna — fastest affinity farm in the game; bring a booster and an XP-sharing frame (Trinity, Equinox)
  • Mot, Void — Steel Path Defense for high-level drops and Steel Essence

⏱️ Survival

Other Missions
  • Stay alive while Life Support drops — Life Support capsules spawn from killed enemies and on the floor
  • Extract every 5 minutes; stay longer for Rotation C (20 min) and D (40 min) rewards
  • Mot, Void — top Steel Path Survival node; Steel Essence + rare mods
  • Nekros (Desecrate) dramatically increases Life Support drops from enemies — bring one per squad
  • Khora (Pilfering Strangledome) doubles resource drops and complements Nekros

🔨 Excavation

Other Missions
  • Protect excavators while they dig for Cryotic — each excavator yields 100 Cryotic when complete
  • Power cells drop from enemies and must be manually carried to power depleted excavators
  • Rotation rewards cycle every 200 Cryotic extracted
  • Hieracon, Pluto — top Excavation node for Cryotic farming and Forma Blueprint drops
  • Cryotic is required for several weapon and Warframe blueprints (Gram, Dakra, etc.)

💪 Interception

Other Missions
  • Capture and hold four towers against enemy capture; maintain 50%+ control to earn points
  • Rotation rewards every full capture cycle; stay for Rotation C for best drops
  • AoE frames (Saryn, Mesa) work well — spread across towers or rotate fast
  • Xini, Eris — top Interception node; Mutagen Samples and Corrupted mods
  • Cerberus, Pluto — Corrupted mod farm alongside Void Fissures

📊 Spy

Other Missions
  • Hack three vault consoles without triggering alarms — each failed vault locks its console for the rest of the mission
  • Rotation rewards require successfully hacking 1, 2, or all 3 vaults (A/B/C)
  • All 3 vaults hacked without alarms = Rotation C reward
  • Warframe recommendations: Ivara (invisibility + no hacking sound), Loki, Ash
  • Kappa, Sedna — Corrupted mods; Lua Spy — Aura mods (Rifle Amp, Energy Siphon, etc.)
  • Gabii, Ceres and Hieracon, Pluto — good mid-game Spy nodes

💥 Kuva Flood & Kuva Siphon

Other Missions
  • Kuva Siphon — standard mission; destroy 4 braid segments to kill the Siphon; rewards 550–700 Kuva
  • Kuva Flood — harder variant with level 80–100 enemies; rewards 1,200 Kuva
  • Run these whenever active on the star chart — Kuva is always needed for Riven rerolling
  • Bring a Kuva Larvling frame to attempt a Kuva Lich spawn after completing (see Lich guide)
  • Queen's Targeter (Naramon focus passive) helps track the Siphon cloud during the mission

🏆 6. Reward Summary

At-a-Glance: Best Mission for Each Goal

Rewards
  • 💲 Credits fast — The Index (High Risk)
  • 🌟 Affinity / leveling — Defense at Hydron (Sedna)
  • 🔨 Cryotic — Excavation at Hieracon (Pluto)
  • 📊 Aura mods — Spy at Lua (Pavlov)
  • 📊 Corrupted mods — Spy at Kappa (Sedna) or Void Fissures
  • 🌟 Riven Mods — Daily Sortie
  • 🔨 Kuva — Kuva Flood (daily); Sorties (occasional)
  • 🎯 Khora — Elite Sanctuary Onslaught (zones 1–4)
  • 🔴 Harrow — Disruption at Kappa (Sedna)
  • 🔴 Gauss — Disruption at Ur (Uranus)
  • ⚡ Archon Shards — Archon Hunt (weekly) or Netracell (weekly)

Daily & Weekly Mission Checklist

Rewards
  • ✅ Sortie — 1 per day; always worth it for Rivens
  • ✅ Archon Hunt — 1 per week; Archon Shards + Archon mods
  • ✅ Netracell — up to 5 per week; Teal/Amber/Violet Archon Shards
  • ✅ ESO — weekly Simaris standing cap; Khora parts if still needed
  • ✅ Index — as needed for Credit top-ups before big crafting sessions
  • ✅ Kuva Flood — whenever active; fast Kuva for Riven rerolls
