📚 1. Overview

What Are Seasonal Events?

Overview
  • Seasonal events are time-limited or recurring missions that offer exclusive rewards not obtainable any other way
  • Most events recur on a rough schedule (every few months to annually) — missing one is rarely permanent, but may mean waiting months
  • Event rewards include: unique mods, Arcanes, frame components, cosmetics, weapon blueprints, and standing currency
  • Check the in-game News panel and the Warframe forums for current event windows
  • Some events award one-time items (first completion only) vs. farmable rewards (repeat runs); know which before committing farm time

Event Calendar Basics

Overview
  • Plague Star: Returns roughly every 2–4 months; check Warframe subreddit/forums for timing
  • Ghoul Purge: Roughly every 2–3 months on the Plains of Eidolon
  • Thermia Fractures: Roughly every 2–3 months on the Orb Vallis
  • Acolytes: Return periodically (historically tied to main updates and anniversaries)
  • Wolf of Saturn Six: Intermittent; Wolf spawns as an assassination target during his active season
  • Resources stockpiled during events (Thermia, Ghoul components) can carry over to future events — hoard them

🦠 2. Plague Star

How It Works

Plague Star
  • Plague Star is a Plains of Eidolon bounty involving Infested enemy waves and a final Hemocyte boss fight
  • Hemocyte: Four-headed infested boss; each head has a specific weak point that must be shot to deal damage
  • The bounty has optional bonus objectives: bringing more Infested Catalyst (Eidolon Phylaxis) boosts bounty rewards
  • Runs take 4–8 minutes; repeat for farming; squads are faster due to multi-head damage distribution
  • Best completed at Night when the Plains is peaceful (no Eidolons spawning)

Key Rewards

Plague Star
  • Plague Zaw parts: Unique Zaw melee components (Plague Kripath, Plague Keewar, etc.) only available during Plague Star — top priority
  • Eidolon Arcanes: Event trading post offers Arcane Energize, Arcane Grace, and others for Operational Supply standing
  • Operational Supply standing is the event currency — earned by completing bounties, spent at Nakak in Cetus
  • Forma Bundle: Forma available for standing — excellent passive gain per event run
  • Plague Zaw parts are the most time-sensitive reward; Arcanes can be obtained from Eidolon hunts year-round

Plague Star Strategy

Plague Star
  • Bring 4 Eidolon Phylaxis (max bonus tier) — craft from Plains resources; gives highest standing reward per run
  • Best frame for Hemocyte: Chroma (Vex Armor damage bonus), Mesa (Peacemakers on all heads), Saryn (Spores spread to all heads)
  • Viral + Slash weapons melt Infested enemies during the wave phase; status-based primaries excel here
  • Hemocyte Cystolith mod drops from the boss — low chance per head but worth collecting if you run many times
  • Run at Tier 4 (4 Phylaxis) every single run during the event window — standing caps fast but Zaw parts are unlimited

🦎 3. Ghoul Purge

How It Works

Ghoul Purge
  • Ghoul Purge adds special Grineer Ghoul enemies to the Plains of Eidolon along with unique bounties
  • Four Ghoul unit types: Auger (burrows), Expired (suicide bombers), Rictus (heavy melee), Devourer (fast and aggressive)
  • Bounties during the event grant Ostron standing bonuses and unique event rewards
  • Ghouls are Grineer faction — standard Grineer weaknesses apply (Viral + Slash or Corrosive + Heat)
  • The event runs for approximately 2 weeks; Ghouls disappear when it ends

Key Rewards

Ghoul Purge
  • Ghoul Saw weapon: A unique circular-saw melee weapon that replaces the primary slot during use; available via event standing
  • Ostron standing bonuses: Bounties during event grant more standing than normal; ideal for ranking up Cetus syndicate
  • Unique mods: Ghoul-specific mods drop from Ghoul enemies (low drop rates but only available during event)
  • Resource drops from Ghoul enemies include Plains-specific materials — useful for Cetus crafting
  • If you already have the Ghoul Saw, prioritize using the event to boost Ostron standing to max rank

🔥 4. Thermia Fractures

How It Works

Thermia
  • Thermia Fractures are geothermal vents that open on the Orb Vallis during the event; seal them to earn Diluted Thermia
  • How to seal: Pick up a Coolant Canister near the fracture and place it on the fracture vent; defend it from Corpus enemies for ~90 seconds
  • Four fractures appear in sequence per cycle; completing all four gives the best Thermia yield
  • Corpus send waves of enemies to destroy placed canisters — AoE frames (Saryn, Equinox, Octavia) are ideal for this event
  • Fractures can be run in a squad for faster sealing and better defense coverage

Key Rewards & Thermia Usage

Thermia
  • Diluted Thermia is the primary output — required to fight the Exploiter Orb boss (see Profit-Taker guide for details)
  • Thermia does not expire between events — stockpile aggressively every time the event runs
  • Recommended stockpile: 40–80 Diluted Thermia gives a substantial reserve for Exploiter farming between events
  • Opticor Vandal blueprint and components were historical Thermia Fractures rewards; check current event reward pool each time it returns
  • Solaris United standing gains during the event from bonus bounties — good time to push Fortuna standing

👻 5. Acolytes

What Are Acolytes?

Acolytes
  • Acolytes are powerful named assassination targets that spawn randomly in missions during an active Acolyte event
  • Six Acolytes: Violence, Malice, Mania, Angst, Torment, Misery — each drops specific mods
  • They spawn mid-mission, alert the player with a distinctive audio cue, and vanish after a time or on player death
  • Acolytes have very high health and damage; they are not designed to be tankable — they are a DPS race
  • Each Acolyte has a global HP pool shared across all players; killing them contributes to their event-wide defeat

Key Mods & Strategy

Acolytes
  • Acolyte mod drops are some of the most valuable mods in the game: Maiming Strike, Pressurized Magazine, Bladed Rounds, Argon Scope, Body Count, Shrapnel Rounds
  • Each Acolyte drops 2 of its specific mods; duplicates happen — sell or trade extras
  • Best strategy: Run high-kill-count missions (Survival, Defense) where Acolytes spawn frequently; bring max DPS — Acolytes flee on player death
  • Viral + high status weapon applies Viral and strips Acolyte DR quickly; melee with high crit is the fastest kill method
  • Acolyte mods that are not dropped during an event can sometimes be obtained via trading from other players — but prices spike between events

Acolyte Drop Table Reference

Acolytes
  • Violence: Maiming Strike, Argon Scope
  • Malice: Focused Defense, Pressurized Magazine
  • Mania: Bladed Rounds, Shrapnel Rounds
  • Angst: Body Count, Repeater Clip
  • Torment: Narrow Barrel, Corrupt Charge
  • Misery: Seeking Shuriken, Wild Frenzy
  • Priority targets: Violence (Maiming Strike), Mania (Bladed Rounds), Angst (Body Count) — all top-tier melee/crit mods

🐺 6. Wolf of Saturn Six

Who Is the Wolf?

Wolf
  • The Wolf of Saturn Six is a massive Grineer assassination target that spawns in random missions during his active season
  • He has extreme health and armor and is almost completely immune to status effects at the time of writing — direct damage only
  • He spawns with two Wolf Hounds (fast melee adds) and becomes more aggressive as his HP drops
  • His attack pattern is slow but hits very hard — keeping distance while dealing damage is the safest approach
  • He can be stunned briefly by knockdown attacks when his heavy attack winds up — useful to interrupt his slam

Wolf Drops & Approach

Wolf
  • Nightwatch Melee Mods: Nightwatch Napalm, Tether Grenades, Nightwatch Tripmines — situational but exclusive
  • Wolf Sledge weapon blueprint & components — rare drops; the Sledge has a unique thrown attack that staggers enemies
  • Best approach: High crit Radiation weapon (Grineer weakness) or Corrosive for armor strip; keep hounds killed to avoid being surrounded
  • Wolf Hounds drop Wolf Sledge components — kill them before focusing Wolf to maximize loot per spawn
  • If the Wolf spawns in a mission you can't finish quickly, consider aborting — he does not respawn in the same session once he flees at low HP

📋 7. Event Prep Checklist

Before Each Event

Prep
  • ✅ Check the News panel in the Orbiter or Warframe forums/subreddit for event start and end date — do not assume it runs forever
  • ✅ Identify which rewards are time-limited one-time (first completion only) vs. farmable (repeat runs) and prioritize accordingly
  • ✅ Confirm daily standing cap for event syndicates — plan your run frequency so you don't waste standing over the cap
  • ✅ Have the right Gear items equipped before entering (Phylaxis for Plague Star, Thermia for Exploiter, Archgun Deployer for Profit-Taker)
  • ✅ Build the event-specific consumables ahead of time if blueprints are available in advance (Eidolon Phylaxis has a 1-hour craft timer — queue before event opens)

General Priority Order

Prep
  • 1st: Grab any one-time exclusive rewards (frame parts, unique weapons) — these never return via other means
  • 2nd: Push syndicate standing to max rank if event grants bonus standing — long-term progression that compounds
  • 3rd: Farm stackable resources (Thermia, Forma from event shop) — higher runs = more future options
  • 4th: Farm tradeable mods or parts — sell or trade for Platinum if you already have them
  • 5th: Cosmetics — last priority unless you specifically want them; cosmetics don't affect gameplay power
  • If time is limited: only complete first-time rewards and move on. Repeat farming can wait for future event returns.
